I was born and raised in Paris, France. In 2007, I completed a degree in Chinese Studies (Philosophy, Literature, and Arts) at [INALCO](http://www.inalco.fr/) (National Institute for Oriental Languages and Civilizations). For the next five years, I worked as a Chinese teacher in several high schools, including a particularly interesting stint at the [Saigon French International High School in Vietnam](http://www.aefe.fr/reseau-scolaire-mondial/rechercher-un-etablissement/viet-nam-ho-chi-minh-ville-lycee-francais).

My journey into the Web3 space began in 2011 when I first heard about Bitcoin. By April 2013, I was experimenting with and designing decentralized apps. Later that year, while working on an iOS payment app, I came across Vitalik Buterin's Ethereum white paper.

In 2014, I joined [OpenClassrooms](https://openclassrooms.com/), one of Europe's leading e-learning companies. There, I set up the customer service department from scratch, managing over 1,000 requests weekly.

2017 marked a significant shift in my career when I became involved with [iExec](https://iex.ec/). I led their crowdsale campaign, raising 10,000 BTC in three hours on [April 19, 2017](https://cointelegraph.com/news/iexec-closes-worlds-5th-largest-ico-with-12-mln-in-6-hours). I then served as Head of Communications for about three years, helping to grow the project's community and visibility.

In 2020, I started [Strat](https://strat.cc), aiming to help build Web3 as a tech stack. During this time, I had the opportunity to teach Marketing Strategy at [EM Lyon](https://www.em-lyon.com/en) and contribute to various Web3 projects, including [Kleros](https://kleros.io/).

That same year, I co-founded [Āto](https://ato.works/), a company focused on providing IP licenses for NFTs and simplifying legal processes for the Web3 community. We developed several NFT-related applications used by artists, auction houses, and fine art galleries.

My experience in Web3 led me to roles as a Developer Relations Engineer for [Aurora](https://aurora.dev/) and [Arthera](https://arthera.net/), where I worked on technical documentation, developer support, and hackathon organization.

In 2023, along with some colleagues and friends, I started the [W3HC](https://w3hc.org/) (Web3 Hackers Collective), a DAO focusing on Web3 integrations, mentoring, and learning. Currently, I'm working on [Gov](https://github.com/w3hc/gov), a DAO framework for regular users.

Throughout my career, I've had the chance to be involved in various interesting projects. One highlight was winning first prize at the [DAO Global Hackathon](https://hackforfreedom.org/#winners) for the [Concord](https://github.com/allforclimate/concord) project. More recently, I was a recipient of the [RetroPGF Round 3](https://vote.optimism.io/retropgf/3/application/0x8ea2ef9ab0d04fe8fdb9edd5208abf0504eafb530378f52b6a401f2b26a9e233) for [Gov](https://github.com/w3hc/gov).

My technical toolkit currently includes [Solidity](https://soliditylang.org/), [Node.js](https://nodejs.org/), [TypeScript](https://www.typescriptlang.org/), and frameworks like [React.js](https://react.dev/), [Next.js](https://nextjs.org/), and [Nest.js](https://nestjs.com/). I recently implemented [the game of Go](https://github.com/julienbrg/game-of-go) in Solidity. You can check out this and my other projects on my [GitHub profile page](https://github.com/julienbrg).

Feel free to reach out to me on [Element](https://matrix.to/#/@julienbrg:matrix.org), [Farcaster](https://warpcast.com/julien-), [Telegram](https://t.me/julienbrg), [Twitter](https://twitter.com/julienbrg), [Discord](https://discordapp.com/users/julienbrg), or [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/julienberanger/)
